Application Rating Prediction Model: Google Play App Store

Smartphones have become an inseparable part of our lives; it has been estimated that an average adult in the U.S. spends almost 3 hours on their smartphone per day, checking their phone once every twelve minutes. 88 percent of time on smartphones are spent on various apps, ranging across categories such as gaming to social networking.

Almost half of all apps are downloaded through app stores ("App Store" for Apple, and "Play Store" for Android). One of the most significant factors in downloading apps are its ratings. Higher ratings have been directly correlated with the number of downloads, and almost 80% of users have reported that they look at an appâ€™s reviews and ratings before choosing which app to download.

For app developers, thus, it is important to predict the type of ratings that their app will receive. Creating apps incurs significant time and money costs, and an app that is poorly rated will be less downloaded and therefore make it more difficult to recoup their costs. Likewise, marketing companies will see great value in having a predictive algorithm for app ratings, for it will directly influence which type of apps these companies will choose to market (either through direct investing or collaboration) and insert advertisements in.

The dataset was obtained from Kaggle. Through an exploratory data analysis, dataset was throughly examined to indentify any abnormal patterns and to deal with missing values. The important features that affect the application ratings in Google Play App Store are identified, and machine learning model that can successfully predict the application rating is developed.
